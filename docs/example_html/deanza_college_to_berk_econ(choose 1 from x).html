Completion of Essential Skills requirements (Reading & Composition, Quantitative Reasoning, and Foreign Language) or

prior to enrollment at UC Berkeley to ensure timely degree progress. For more information see the see the New Policies

WHY STUDY ECONOMICS?


Have you ever thought, what can I do with a degree in Economics?


Economics can be broadly defined as the study of human behaviors aimed at finding solutions to help improve peoples' lives. A

degree in economics doesn't have to be about finance, banking, business, or government, . . . it can be useful to all individuals

and can lead to many interesting and fulfilling career choices.

exam credit

  1. Advanced Placement Examinations (AP Credit)
  2. Math AB - scores of 3, 4 or 5 are equivalent to Math 16A or Math 1A.
  3. Math BC - scores of 3 or 4 are equivalent to Math 16A or Math 1A; a score of 5 is equivalent to Math 1B and fulfills the Economics calculus prerequisite.
  4. Economics Micro/Macro - scores of 4 or 5 on both exams are equivalent to Economics 1 or Economics 2.
  5. GCE Advanced Level Examinations (A Level Credit)
  6. Mathematics - score of A, B, or C is equivalent to Math 1A and 1B and fulfills the Economics calculus prerequisite.
  7. Economics Micro/Macro - score of A, B, or C is equivalent to Econ 1 and fulfills the Introductory Economics prerequisite. Students may enroll in upper division economics coursework beginning in the second semester of their freshman year.
  8. International Baccalaureate Higher Level Examinations (IB Credit)
  9. Mathematics - score of 5, 6, or 7 fulfills the Economics calculus prerequisite.
  10. Mathematics: Analysis & Approaches - score of 5, 6, or 7 fulfills MATH 1A or MATH 16A.
  11. Mathematics: Analysis & Approaches - score of 7 fulfills MATH 1A and MATH 1B.
  12. Mathematics: Applications & Interpretation - score of 5, 6, or 7 fulfills MATH 16A.
  13. Economics Micro/Macro - score of 5, 6, or 7 is equivalent to Econ 1 and fulfills the Introductory Economics prerequisite.
